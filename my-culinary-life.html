An Uncommon Friend-Joe Colanero

I couldn’t name another culinary friend from CSP who touched my life so deeply as the
late Joe Colanero. He was talented but modest; gentle yet articulate. What was most
striking was his enduring interest in food and people. We were connected in a very
special way from the start. The passion for food knowledge and entertaining we shared,
and the support we derived from each other was remarkable.

The best part was that both of our spouses also enjoyed the friendship. Not only did we invite each other to home parties and dining out, but we even included friends from both sides. Furthermore, he was always there during my book promotions; and when it was his turn to promote his cookbook, my husband and I held home parties to sell his book.

The most significant of our collaboration was the special role we played
-as vegetarian chefs at the Philadelphia Food Convention during the late 90’s. It was such exciting experience to feed a crowd of almost a thousand visitors and hear the continuous praise of our meatless feast. Even to this day when I occasionally chat with Joe’s widow, Lorreta, I still savor the fabulous feeling of these past events.
